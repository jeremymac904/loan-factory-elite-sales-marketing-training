## 2. Key Research Findings by Topic

### 2.1 Mortgage Sales and Production Systems

**Finding.** Todd Duncan, who personally originated more than 5,000 transactions in 12 years and built High Trust Selling, frames the core decision as "where do I place my selling confidence?" His answer is that confidence shifts from "sell" to "help." He also reframes prospecting from chasing loans to building Realtor relationships: "I could go look for 10 loans from 10 realtors, or I could look for one realtor that can give me 10 loans" (toddduncan.com, "What is High Trust Selling?").

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** Most loan officers chase transactions and never build the partner base that produces predictable volume. A help first frame also keeps the LO out of teaser rate territory and Reg Z trouble.

**How it shows up in training.** Every module opens with the same selling philosophy: we help, we do not pitch. Module 301 is built around the one Realtor who can give me 10 loans model rather than 10 cold Realtor coffees a week.

### 2.2 Borrower Conversion and Speed to Lead

**Finding.** Dr. James Oldroyd, then a Faculty Fellow at MIT's Sloan School of Management, partnered with InsideSales.com to study 15,000+ leads and 100,000+ call attempts across six companies over three years. The findings were popularized by Harvard Business Review in 2011 ("The Short Life of Online Sales Leads"). The study found firms responding within five minutes were 100 times more likely to make contact and 21 times more likely to qualify a lead than firms that waited 30 minutes. Velocify's study of 3.5 million leads found calling within one minute increased conversion by 391%. Per HubSpot, 35 to 50% of sales go to the vendor that responds first.

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** Most lost leads were never lost on price. They were lost in the first hour. A new LO with mediocre product knowledge but a five minute response time will out convert a senior LO who answers four hours late.

**How it shows up in training.** 201 builds a five minute speed to lead drill. 501 builds the AI assisted first touch workflow (auto text, calendar link, prep brief) so the LO is reachable even when in another appointment.

### 2.3 Follow Up Cadence

**Finding.** Per RAIN Group and corroborating B2B sales research, average meeting acquisition requires 8 touches, while 44% of reps quit after one follow up. Across multiple aggregated studies, 5 to 8 multi channel touches (call, email, text, LinkedIn) over 10 to 21 days is the high performance range, and most replies come between touches 5 and 8.

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** LOs typically quit after touch 2. They are leaving the majority of their pipeline on the table at the exact point where replies start to compound.

**How it shows up in training.** 101 teaches a simple 7 touch cadence over 14 days. 501 teaches advanced multi channel cadences and AI assisted personalization for the 30, 60, 90 day window.

### 2.4 High Trust Selling and Behavioral Psychology

**Finding.** Cialdini's seven principles (reciprocity, commitment and consistency, social proof, authority, liking, scarcity, unity) all show up in trust driven sales. Kitces specifically recommends that advisors "let prospects test drive the client experience" as a reciprocity move that "can demonstrate the advisor's value and skill." On sales conversation mechanics, per Gong's March 20, 2025 blog update by Dan Morgese analyzing 326,000 sales calls, the average rep talks 60% of the time and "The golden ratio for sales success was 43% talking to 57% listening." Gong's 2025 analysis also found that "sellers who won deals asked 15 to 16 questions in their calls, while sellers who lost deals asked more questions — about 20 per call." More questions does not equal better; better questions does.

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** Trust is built through asking, listening, and giving something useful before requesting commitment. A mortgage LO who talks 70% of the first call has already lost it. A mortgage LO who fires 25 rapid questions has interrogated the borrower.

**How it shows up in training.** 201 installs a borrower intake template with a target talk to listen ratio of 43:57 and a discovery target of 15 to 16 high quality questions, not a quota of 25. The free preview reciprocity move shows up as a complimentary loan options review the LO offers before asking for the application.

### 2.5 Referral Partner Growth

**Finding.** NAR's 2024 Profile of Home Buyers and Sellers (data collected July 2023 to June 2024) shows that 40% of buyers used an agent they found through a referral and 21% used an agent they had worked with in the past; 88% of buyers would use their agent again. Inman survey data cited by PNC reports that "speed ranked as the highest factor that determines whether an agent will recommend a lender to a client, with 59% of respondents citing it as the most important." Agents care most about responsiveness 7 days a week, fast pre approval turnaround, on time closings, clear communication, and listing agent calls that confirm financing strength.

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** Realtors do not refer based on rate sheets. They refer based on closing certainty and how the LO makes the agent look in front of their seller and listing agent.

**How it shows up in training.** 301 teaches a listing agent call workflow, weekly proactive status updates, and a Realtor value prop that leads with speed and communication, not rate.

### 2.6 Content Marketing and Local Authority

**Finding.** Google's official guidance on local rankings names three factors: relevance, distance, and prominence. Reviews and review velocity, profile completeness, and engagement signals (calls, direction requests, website clicks) are key levers. The 2026 Local Search Ranking Factors survey from Whitespark identifies on page signals, Google Business Profile completeness, reviews, and citations as the largest categories. Per Backlinko's 2026 local SEO guide, the local map pack is driven by relevance, distance, and prominence and is the highest intent inbound channel for a local professional.

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** A Loan Factory LO who is fully optimized on Google Business Profile in their farm city with weekly posts and a steady review velocity will appear in the local pack while competitors spend on paid leads.

**How it shows up in training.** 401 includes a Google Business Profile build checklist, weekly post template, and a review request workflow tied to closed loans.

### 2.7 Short Form Video Strategy

**Finding.** Aggregated platform and creator tool data from 2025 to 2026 (OpusClip, Sendspark, multiple platform best practices guides) shows the first 3 seconds drive approximately 80% of completion variance on Reels, TikTok, and Shorts. Instagram has publicly identified three Reels priority ranking signals: watch time, likes per reach, and sends per reach. 85% of users watch with sound off, making captions critical. Daily cadence beats weekly excellence in the first 30 days for algorithm learning. Hook patterns that perform: bold contrarian statement, specific question to the target audience, problem solution. Nike's TikTok problem solution campaign Q2 2025 produced 48% higher retention per DriveEditor's 2025 trends review.

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** Most LOs make videos that open with "Hi, I'm Bob, I'm a loan officer." That loses the viewer in 1.5 seconds. A 3 second hook tied to a borrower pain point keeps them.

**How it shows up in training.** 401 trains LOs on a 5 hooks every LO should master framework, with a 60 second video script template they can shoot on their phone.

### 2.8 Database, Past Client, and Refinance Retention

**Finding.** Mortgage industry retention is brutal. Per Black Knight Mortgage Monitor data cited by Homebot's interview with Charlie Pratt, the industry barely retains 20% of past customers. Per Frederick Reichheld of Bain & Company in Harvard Business Review (October 2014, "The Value of Keeping the Right Customers"), "increasing customer retention rates by 5% increases profits by 25% to 95%." Per J.D. Power's 2024 U.S. Mortgage Origination Satisfaction Study (7,534 respondents, August 2023 to September 2024), "when local brand representatives are directly involved in the mortgage origination process, overall satisfaction rises 40 points" and "the satisfaction score for trust among borrowers who strongly rely on the lender's expertise to get through the borrowing process is 133 points higher than among those borrowers who do not strongly rely on the lender's expertise."

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** Every closed Loan Factory file is a 5 to 10 year annuity if the LO stays in front of the client. Most LOs let that annuity walk. The economic payoff is asymmetric: a 5 point lift in retention can move profit by 25 to 95%.

**How it shows up in training.** 501 builds a past client routine: equity check at 6 months, anniversary letter at 12 months, refi screen at every meaningful rate move, value first content monthly. AI assisted personalization, never robotic.

### 2.9 AI Assisted Workflows

**Finding.** McKinsey research puts personalization expectation at 71% and frustration with the lack of it at 76%. Per industry specific reviews (FinLocker AI toolkit, OnCourse Learning's Top 10 AI Tools for Loan Officers, Shape Software's review), the highest ROI AI plays for LOs in 2025 are: first touch response and qualification, content drafting (social, email, GBP posts), call prep briefs, objection roleplay, and pipeline review summaries. **Why it matters for LF LOs.** AI lets a busy LO behave like a 5 person team without sounding fake. The risk is using it to spam or to publish content with triggering terms.

**How it shows up in training.** Every module ends with one specific AI prompt the LO actually uses that week. 601 installs the full AI workflow stack: prep, follow up, content, roleplay, weekly review.

### 2.10 Coaching, Accountability, and Adult Learning

**Finding.** Anders Ericsson's deliberate practice research (Psychological Review, 1993) shows expert performance is built by structured, focused, feedback rich repetition on specific weaknesses, not hours of unfocused work. BJ Fogg's Tiny Habits (Stanford Behavior Design Lab) shows behavior change is most reliable when designed as B=MAP: a behavior occurs when motivation, ability, and a prompt converge, and tiny habits stack on top of existing routines.

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** "Try harder" does not work. "Send 5 follow up texts right after your morning coffee" does.

**How it shows up in training.** Every assignment is a tiny habit tied to an existing trigger. Coaching is structured around weekly roleplay and one specific behavior at a time.

### 2.11 Compliance and Risk

**Finding.** Regulation Z (12 C.F.R. § 1026) defines an advertisement broadly. Per Allison Burns, Senior Examiner at the Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis writing in Consumer Compliance Outlook (2021), "Regulation Z broadly defines advertisements as commercial messages provided in any medium that promote ― directly or indirectly ― a credit transaction." Triggering terms in closed end mortgage credit are: a specific down payment, a payment period, a specific payment amount, or a specific finance charge. When any triggering term appears, the ad must also disclose the amount or percentage of any down payment, the terms of repayment over the full term including any balloon, and the APR using that term. The interest rate may be listed but not more conspicuously than the APR.

The FFIEC's 2013 interagency Social Media Guidance, hosted on consumerfinance.gov, makes clear that existing consumer protection and compliance laws and regulations apply to activities conducted by financial institutions through social media as they would to activities conducted through other channels (FDIC FIL-56-2013). The SAFE Act requires that each licensed MLO must prominently display their NMLS ID on all advertising materials and public communications, including social profiles and posts.

On RESPA Section 8, the CFPB's October 2020 RESPA FAQs make clear that an MSA "is or can become unlawful if the facts and circumstances show that the MSA as structured, or the parties' implementation of the MSA involves an agreement to pay for referrals." The CFPB's 2023 enforcement action (the first public RESPA Section 8 enforcement since 2017) targeted a lender paying for subscription services and events provided to Realtors. Co marketing remains permissible if both parties pay their pro rata share for any joint advertising and no thing of value is exchanged for referrals (HUD 2016 FAQ #18, cited by NAR and RISMedia).

**Why it matters for LF LOs.** A single Instagram reel saying "Get a 5.99% rate, only $1,500 down, payments as low as $2,100/month" without APR and other disclosures is a Reg Z violation. A Starbucks card to a Realtor with the words thanks for the referral is a RESPA Section 8 problem.

**How it shows up in training.** Every public, borrower facing, or Realtor facing artifact is flagged COMPLIANCE REVIEW. 401 includes a safe content decision tree. 301 includes a what you can and cannot do with Realtors one pager.

## 3. What Most Sales Training Gets Wrong for Mortgage LOs

1. **Motivational fluff with no execution system.** Conferences leave LOs hyped on Monday and unchanged on Friday. Loan Factory training is built around weekly trackers and assignments, not slogans.
2. **Generic B2B sales frameworks.** SPIN, MEDDIC, Challenger are useful conceptually but were not built for a 21 to 45 day mortgage transaction with a Realtor in the middle. Loan Factory teaches mortgage specific application.
3. **Cold calling Realtors at random.** Walking into an open house cold with cookies works rarely. 301 teaches a targeted Realtor strategy: identify, research, give value, ask for a working session, become indispensable on a live deal.
4. **Rate led conversations.** Leading with rate creates teaser rate compliance risk and trains the borrower to shop on price. 201 teaches a structure that leads with goals, plan, options, and only then numbers.
5. **Posting daily for vanity.** Posting six times a day on six platforms with no hook and no positioning does nothing. 401 teaches one focused content pillar per LO and a hook framework.
6. **Treating database marketing as monthly email blasts.** That trains clients to ignore the LO. 501 teaches a value first cadence.
7. **Using AI to generate generic content that sounds fake.** That hurts trust. Every AI prompt in 601 is built to sound like the LO.
8. **Asking more questions thinking it equals better discovery.** Per Gong 2025, losers asked about 20 questions per call, winners 15 to 16. Better questions, not more.

### 6.4 Sales Math
Working backward from an income goal:
1. Annual income goal divided by average revenue per closing equals closings needed.
2. Closings needed divided by closing rate equals applications needed.

4. Pre quals needed divided by conversation conversion equals conversations needed.
5. Divide by 50 working weeks to get weekly conversation targets.

For most new LF LOs, the answer lands at 5 to 8 real conversations per business day.

## 7. Full Module Detail for 201: Borrower Conversion

### 7.1 First Call Structure (the LF High Trust Intake)
1. Open warm, set the agenda, ask permission. 60 seconds.
2. Goals discovery. What are you trying to accomplish? Why now? What would the right outcome look like in 6 months?
3. Current situation. Income picture, credit comfort, savings comfort, timeline.
4. Plan. Walk back what you heard. Outline the next steps.
5. Options. Talk in terms of paths and tradeoffs, not specific rates.
6. Next step. Book the next call. Confirm document checklist. Confirm pre approval target date.

Talk to listen target: 43:57 in favor of listening (Gong 2025 golden ratio). Ask 15 to 16 quality questions on the first call (Gong 2025 winners benchmark). Avoid 20+ rapid fire questions; that is what losers do.

### 7.2 Building Trust Fast
- Reciprocity (Cialdini): offer something useful before asking for the file (a one page plan, a market snapshot, a budget calculator review).
- Authority: lead with your role, brokerage, and lender access calmly. Never brag.
- Consistency: end the call with a small, easy commitment (send the W 2, confirm next call), then escalate.

### 7.3 Rate and Fee Conversation Without Teaser Talk
Reg Z says the moment you put a specific rate, payment, down payment, or fee in writing in a public message, you trigger additional disclosures. In live conversation, you have more flexibility, but the LF rule is the same: anchor on the borrower's plan and total monthly cost, not a teaser headline.

Rate question redirect script:

```
Great question. Rates move daily and depend on file specifics like credit, down payment, property type, and lock period. Here's what I'd rather do: let me build two or three real options on your actual file and walk you through the tradeoffs. That way you're comparing real numbers, not headline rates. Fair?
```

Fee question redirect script:

```
Fair question. On a Loan Factory file, you'll see a clear Loan Estimate within three business days of application. I'd rather show you the actual numbers than guess. Want to do the quick app today so I can show you Wednesday?
```

### 7.4 Next Step Control
Always end with a next step that has a date and time. Calendar link, document upload, or a confirmed call back. The Oldroyd/InsideSales five minute rule means the conversion drop off curve is steepest in the first hour. Lock in next contact while you are on the phone.

### 7.5 201 Assignment

## 8. Full Module Detail for 301: Referral Partner Growth

### 8.1 Realtor Outreach Strategy
The Inman survey cited by PNC found "speed ranked as the highest factor that determines whether an agent will recommend a lender to a client, with 59% of respondents citing it as the most important." Build the outreach around what Realtors actually care about: responsiveness, pre approval speed, on time closings, clear communication, listing agent calls.

Lead with three commitments, in this order:

### 8.3 First Meeting Structure
1. Ask first. Their business, their pain on the financing side, their last failed deal.
2. Connect. Where you can specifically help.
3. Offer one small test. A live pre approval, a co hosted first time buyer class (RESPA pro rata, see compliance), a listing agent call on their next offer.

### 8.4 Co Marketing Within RESPA
Per HUD's 2016 FAQ #18 cited by NAR and RISMedia, "Nothing in RESPA prevents joint advertising. However, if one party is paying less than a pro rata share for the brochure or advertisement, there could be a RESPA violation." The CFPB's October 2020 RESPA FAQs warn that an MSA can be unlawful if it "involves an agreement to pay for referrals" or pays in excess of fair market value. The 2023 CFPB enforcement action specifically named subscription services, free event tickets, and ill structured MSAs as kickback violations.

Loan Factory rule for 301:
- Joint flyers and joint open house signage are fine if both parties pay pro rata.
- No gifts of value tied to referrals. No Starbucks cards for sending a lead. No paying for the agent's MLS or CRM.
- MSAs require corporate approval. DECISION NEEDED for any new MSA.
- Document fair market value for everything paid.
