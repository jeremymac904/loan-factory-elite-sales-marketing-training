# Competitor Intelligence Roadmap

Internal strategy roadmap for the Loan Factory LO Development Platform. Tracks what the market is doing — retail lenders, large brokerages, recruiting offers, coaching platforms, and AI/LO tools — and turns each signal into a decision: what we monitor, why it matters to our platform, and what we build or teach in response.

## What this is

A living competitor-intelligence roadmap for the LO Development Platform. It is organized into five monitoring categories. Each category has three parts: **What to monitor**, **Why it matters to our platform**, and **What Loan Factory could build or teach in response**. Every concrete external claim (an offer, a feature, a price) carries a source URL and an access date, or is explicitly marked `SOURCE NEEDED`.

This is an internal roadmap, not marketing copy. It contains no borrower-facing claims, no rate/fee/APR data, and no superlatives about Loan Factory.

## Who it's for

- **Jeremy (master_admin):** platform build and prioritization decisions.
- **Andre (LO Development) and the LO Development team:** what to emphasize in recruiting conversations and member ramp.
- **Edward (corporate coaching):** how competitor coaching offers should shape our LO Mastery and Loan Factory Alliance curriculum.
- **Marketing reviewer (Victoria):** context for any recruiting or comparison language before it ships.

Loan Factory is a wholesale/broker-channel shop. Frame everything wholesale-only. Do not propose correspondent lending as a response to any competitor move.

## How to use this doc

1. **Read by category.** Pick the section that matches a decision you are making (e.g., a recruiting conversation -> Category 3).
2. **Trust only cited claims.** If a line says `SOURCE NEEDED`, treat it as an unverified rumor until someone attaches a source URL and an access date. Do not put it in a deck or a recruiting message.
3. **Refresh on cadence.** See "Monitoring cadence" below. Competitor offers and AI features move fast; a claim with a stale access date is a liability, not an asset.
4. **Translate signal into platform work.** Each "build or teach in response" item is a candidate for the LO Development build queue or the 101-601 / coaching curriculum. It is a starting point, not an approved spec.
5. **Never weaponize a number you cannot defend.** When we cite a competitor split or price, we cite the source out loud. No "lowest", "best", or "guaranteed" framing about Loan Factory anywhere.

## Category 1 — Retail lenders (call-center and direct-to-consumer model)

Retail giants are the loudest voice in the market and the model our broker-channel LOs are most often compared against. Their moves set borrower and LO expectations.

### What to monitor

- **Rocket Companies' AI stack.** Rocket Logic is a patented AI-driven platform combining proprietary data and call-transcript volume with deep-learning and generative-AI systems; Rocket has stated AI prospecting cut LO prospecting time substantially while lifting conversion. Track each new capability release. [SOURCE: Rocket Companies press release, "Rocket Companies Introduces Rocket Logic AI Platform", https://www.rocketcompanies.com/press-release/rocket-companies-introduces-rocket-logic-ai-platform-to-make-homeownership-faster-and-easier/ — accessed 2026-05-28]
- **Rocket's fully digital, no-LO-touch flows.** Rocket launched fully digital purchase pre-approvals (Feb 2026), built on a fully digital refinance experience (Jul 2025), positioned as freeing LOs for higher-value clients. [SOURCE: National Mortgage News, "Rocket Mortgage rolls out AI-powered platform for underwriting", https://www.nationalmortgagenews.com/news/rocket-mortgage-rolls-out-ai-powered-platform-for-underwriting — accessed 2026-05-28. NOTE: confirm exact pre-approval launch date against a Rocket primary source before quoting it in any deck. SOURCE NEEDED: Rocket primary-source confirmation of the Feb 2026 digital purchase pre-approval date.]
- **Rocket's Q4 2025 / 2026 positioning** of Rocket Pro as a growth engine and the Redfin tie-in for demand. [SOURCE: PRNewswire, "Rocket Companies Announces Fourth Quarter and Full Year 2025 Results", https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/rocket-companies-announces-fourth-quarter-and-full-year-2025-results-302698920.html — accessed 2026-05-28]
- Retail recruiting ads aimed at broker-channel LOs (signing bonuses, lead guarantees, "we do the marketing for you" messaging). `SOURCE NEEDED: specific current retail recruiting offers — capture screenshots with URLs and dates as they appear.`

### Why it matters to our platform

Retail competes on perceived speed and "the brand does the work." When an LO is recruited away or a borrower compares experiences, the implicit pitch is "the technology replaces the loan officer." Our LO Development Platform's entire thesis is the opposite: the loan officer plus AI enablement (the TERA-aligned AI Twin, the AI Coaching Assistant, the 101-601 curriculum) is the durable advantage. We need to know exactly what retail AI claims to do so our training answers it honestly and specifically, without disparaging a named competitor with unverified facts (see COMPLIANCE_NOTES.md).

### What Loan Factory could build or teach in response

- A 201/501 module: "Where AI replaces tasks vs. where the loan officer wins" — concrete, non-borrower-facing talk tracks an LO can use when a borrower mentions a fully digital retail flow. Compliance review required; no comparison to a named competitor with unverified facts.
- AI Assistant Hub parity check: maintain an internal feature comparison (our AI Twin + AI Coaching Assistant capabilities vs. publicly announced retail AI features) so we know where we are genuinely strong and where we have a gap.
- A recruiting talking-points sheet for the LO Development team that frames the broker-channel value proposition against the retail "we do it all" pitch — wholesale-only, source-disciplined, no superlatives.

## Category 2 — Large mortgage brokerages (mega brokers)

These are our most direct recruiting competitors: broker-channel shops at 1,000+ LO scale fighting for the same loan officers.

### What to monitor

- **Mega-broker scale and definitions.** NEXA Mortgage is reported as the largest broker by LO count (reported at 2,000+ LOs); a "mega brokerage" is defined by NEXA CEO Mike Kortas as 1,000+ loan officers. Other named mega brokers include Edge Home Finance and C2 Financial. - **Broker channel headcount trend.** The broker-channel LO count was reported rising ~12.5% year over year to ~56,803, while still the smallest LO segment overall. [SOURCE: HousingWire, "Producing loan officers rise in 2025 as mortgage market stabilizes", https://www.housingwire.com/articles/loan-officer-growth-2025/ — accessed 2026-05-28. NOTE: 403 to automated fetch; re-confirm the 12.5% / 56,803 figures against the live article.]
- **New broker platforms from ex-retail leaders.** Origna8.com — a new broker platform tied to former Rocket Pro executive Mike Fawaz, packaging technology, marketing, recruiting, leads, and lender partnerships "under one roof." [SOURCE: HousingWire, "Mike Fawaz partners with UWM on new broker platform", https://www.housingwire.com/articles/fawaz-uwm-broker-platform/ — accessed 2026-05-28]
- Scotsman Guide rankings for top originators and top brokerages, refreshed annually. [SOURCE: Scotsman Guide rankings, https://www.scotsmanguide.com/rankings/ — accessed 2026-05-28]
- `SOURCE NEEDED: each named mega broker's current published recruiting/onboarding offer and tech stack — capture per competitor with URL and date.`

### Why it matters to our platform

Loan Factory recruits in the same pool. The mega brokers are bundling tech + leads + recruiting + lender access into one pitch (Origna8 is the clearest example, and it is UWM-aligned). Our LO Development Platform is, in effect, our differentiated bundle: structured 101-601 development, paid coaching tiers (LO Mastery, Loan Factory Alliance), AI enablement, Team Leader OS, and the 1+1=5 team-growth system. We need to know precisely what the competing bundles include so the platform's "front door from day one to elite producer to team leader to coach" story is sharp and specific.

### What Loan Factory could build or teach in response

- An internal, single-page "platform bundle" comparison the LO Development team can reference when a recruit names a specific mega broker — built from sourced facts only, refreshed on cadence.
- Lean into what a one-roof internal platform uniquely offers a broker-channel LO: a structured development path and accountability (1+1=5, Team Leader OS) that a pure "tech + leads" bundle does not provide. Teach this in the team-leader track, not as a borrower claim.
- Track which mega brokers are publicly building recruiting/revenue-share mechanics (Category 3) so our coaching content on team growth stays current.

## Category 3 — Advertised recruiting offers and commission splits

This is the most volatile and most weaponized category in LO recruiting. Numbers here change fast and are often quoted out of context, so source discipline matters most.

### What to monitor

- **NEXA100 (100% commission model).** NEXA markets a plan letting LOs keep "up to 100% of their commission splits" with no per-file or hidden fees, plus a revenue-share program. [SOURCE: MPA, "No more commission cuts? NEXA offers loan officers 100% splits", https://www.mpamag.com/us/news/general/no-more-commission-cuts-nexa-offers-loan-officers-100-splits/490954 — accessed 2026-05-28; article dated 2024-05-28; CEO Mike Kortas quoted.]
  - The frequently circulated worked example (e.g., on a $500,000 loan the LO nets ~$11,000 after NEXA deducts ~25 bps for funding and a ~12% margin) and the "10 bps per recruited LO's funded loan" revenue-share figure appear in secondary coverage but were not verified in a primary NEXA source for this doc. `SOURCE NEEDED: primary NEXA source (or the live NMP article at https://nationalmortgageprofessional.com/news/nexa-pays-loan-officers-100-commission-splits, which returned 403) confirming the bps deductions, margin, and revenue-share rate before any of these numbers are quoted to a recruit.`
- **Flat-fee / per-file recruiting offers.** Examples in market: Innovative Mortgage (originator + team leader share 100% of elected comp less ~$695 per file) and Simply Approved Mortgages (~$1,250/month flat fee, 100% earnings as 1099). [SOURCE: search summary referencing innovativemlo.com and simplyapprovedmortgages.com — accessed 2026-05-28. NOTE: re-confirm both numbers on the named companies' own pages before use; capture the page URL and date at confirmation.]
- **Lender-side broker credits used as recruiting leverage.** Rocket Pro announced an added broker pricing credit tied to a Compass partnership at Ignite26 (reported as 40 bps). [SOURCE: HousingWire, "Rocket Pro Compass partnership adds 40 bps broker credit", https://www.housingwire.com/articles/rocket-pro-compass-40bps-jupiter/ — accessed 2026-05-28. NOTE: 403 to automated fetch; confirm the 40 bps figure and what it applies to against the live article or a Rocket primary source.]
- `SOURCE NEEDED: current signing bonuses, draw guarantees, and lead-credit offers from named competitors — these are the highest-churn numbers; recapture monthly.`

### Why it matters to our platform

Recruiting conversations live or die on comp math, and our LO Development team will be asked to respond to "Brokerage X pays 100%." The platform's job is not to win a race to the lowest cost — it is to make the *total* value (development path, coaching, AI enablement, team growth) legible against a single headline split number. Internally we must have the competitor offer documented accurately so we never overstate it (which makes us look uninformed) or misstate it (which is a compliance and credibility risk).

### What Loan Factory could build or teach in response

- A sourced, internal "recruiting offer ledger" (one row per competitor, columns: offer, fees, revenue share, source URL, access date) that the LO Development team can pull from `/admin`-side tooling. Keep it internal and source-stamped; never publish externally.
- Coaching content (LO Mastery / Loan Factory Alliance) on how a producing LO should evaluate a comp offer beyond the headline split: ramp support, technology, lead flow, accountability, and career path. No claim that Loan Factory is "best" or "lowest" — teach the evaluation framework, let the LO conclude.
- A Team Leader OS module on building durable team economics (1+1=5) so team leaders compete on growth systems, not just split headlines.

- Emphasize the integrated, compliance-first AI story as a structural advantage: free competitor tools that auto-populate fields or auto-send content create compliance exposure we deliberately avoid. Position this internally; no superlative or guarantee language.
- Watch CRM-side AI (Aidium, Surefire) for content/compliance features we should mirror in the AI Assistant Hub's compliance-precheck assistant.

## Source + date discipline

Non-negotiable rules for this doc and anything derived from it:

1. **Every concrete external claim carries a source URL and an access date.** Format: `[SOURCE: publication, "title", URL — accessed YYYY-MM-DD]`. If you cannot produce both, mark the line `SOURCE NEEDED: <exactly what is missing>`.
2. **Access date is when we last verified the claim against the source**, not when the source was published. Show the publication date too when it is known (offers and prices age quickly).
3. **Secondary coverage is not a primary source.** Roundup blogs and news summaries are fine for *finding* a claim; before a number goes into a recruiting or coaching artifact, confirm it on the competitor's own page or filing and re-stamp the date. Several sources here (NMP, HousingWire articles) returned 403 to automated fetch — those figures are from search summaries and are explicitly flagged for re-confirmation.
4. **No invented facts.** We do not estimate a competitor's split, fee, AI capability, or price. If it is not sourced, it is `SOURCE NEEDED`.
5. **No borrower-facing claims, ever.** This roadmap is internal. Nothing here is borrower-facing copy, and no rate/fee/APR data appears in it.
6. **No superlatives about Loan Factory.** Never "lowest", "best", or "guaranteed." We document competitor offers accurately and position Loan Factory on integrated value, not comparative claims.
7. **Named-competitor comparisons follow compliance.** Per COMPLIANCE_NOTES.md, no direct comparison to a named competitor using unverified facts. When we do compare, the facts are sourced and dated, and the framing is internal-only.
8. **Wholesale-only framing.** Loan Factory is broker/wholesale channel. No response in this roadmap proposes correspondent lending.
9. **Automation stays manual-first.** Nothing here instructs anyone to run live n8n workflows, auto-send messages, upload videos, or otherwise bypass the manual-first / review-required posture.
