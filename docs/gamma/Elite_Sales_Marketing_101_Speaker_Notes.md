# Elite Sales and Marketing Training 101
## Speaker Notes. In Jeremy's voice.

Plain English. Confident. Practical. Short sentences. No corporate fluff. Includes what to say live, when to pause, the Q and A question, and what to listen for.

---

## Slide 1. Title

**Say live:**
Hey team. Welcome to 101. The point of today is simple. We are not adding more to your day. We are organizing the day you already have. By the end of this hour, you walk out with one system you can run starting Monday.

**Live tone:** Calm. Direct.

**Pause point:** None.

**Closing beat:** Tell the room you are going to repeat the core line a few times today on purpose. Repetition is the operating system.

---

## Slide 2. The real problem

**Say live:**
Most LOs in this room are not lazy. You are working hard. You are just scattered. Different leads from different places. Different scripts. Different follow up patterns. Some weeks five conversations a day. Other weeks zero. Sound familiar? That is the problem.

**Live tone:** Acknowledge the work. Then name the problem.

**Pause point:** None.

**Closing beat:** Tell them the answer is not more effort. The answer is one simple weekly system anyone can run.

---

## Slide 3. The 101 operating principle

**Say live:**
Here is the spine of everything we do in this series. Conversations create pipeline. Pipeline creates closings. Closings create referrals. Track the work that creates conversations. That is it. We will come back to this line all six sessions.

**Live tone:** Slow. Repeat the chain twice.

**Pause point:**
PAUSE FOR LIVE Q AND A.
Question: What feels scattered in your current sales and marketing routine?

**Listen for:**
1. Lead sources they cannot reach.
2. Follow up that goes silent at touch two.
3. Realtors they have not talked to in a month.
4. No tracker. They are guessing.

**Closing beat:** Repeat the chain one more time before you move on. Conversations. Pipeline. Closings. Referrals.

---

## Slide 4. Professional advisor, not rate order taker

**Say live:**
Two ways to work this job. Order taker. Advisor. Order takers quote a rate to a stranger and hope it sticks. Advisors get the goal, the timeline, and the plan first. Numbers come after the plan, not before. The whole series is built on the advisor path.

**Live tone:** Firm. This is a habit fix.

**Pause point:** None.

**Compliance beat:** Remind the room. Live conversations have more flexibility. The minute you put a specific rate, payment, or fee into writing in public, that is a Reg Z advertisement. Disclosures or rewrite.

---

## Slide 5. The broker model in plain English

**Say live:**
Loan Factory is a mortgage brokerage. Plain English. We have access to more than 240 wholesale lenders. That gives us choice, fit, and flexibility. We do not bash banks. We do not bash retail. We talk about what more options means for the borrower.

**Live tone:** Calm. Confident. Not salesy.

**Pause point:**
PAUSE FOR LIVE Q AND A.
Question: Can you explain the broker model in plain English without sounding salesy?

**Listen for:**
1. Choice, fit, flexibility.
2. No specific savings claim.
3. No competitor bashing.
4. Borrower benefit framed in plain language.

**Closing beat:** Practice the 30 second broker value prop out loud twice as a homework primer.

---

## Slide 6. The first borrower conversation

**Say live:**
The application link is not the relationship. A real conversation builds trust. A real conversation catches credit, income, and timeline issues you cannot see in the app. A real conversation creates a cleaner file at submission. Stop dropping links and walking away. Make the call first.

**Live tone:** Concrete. Use a story if you have one.

**Pause point:**
PAUSE FOR LIVE Q AND A.
Question: What happens when a borrower gets only a link and no real conversation?

**Listen for:**
1. They go quiet.
2. Missing documents.
3. Credit surprises late.
4. Shopping you with three other lenders.

**Closing beat:** Use the borrower first text and voicemail scripts you got in the 101 handout.

---

## Slide 7. The first referral partner conversation

**Say live:**
Realtors do not refer based on rate sheets. They refer based on certainty. Speed. Communication. Clean pre approvals. Listing agent calls on every offer. Lead with usefulness. Find one specific thing about their business. Offer 15 minutes. Do not beg for referrals.

**Live tone:** Peer to peer. Adult professional.

**Pause point:**
PAUSE FOR LIVE Q AND A.
Question: What would make a Realtor trust you enough to send one real buyer?

**Listen for:**
1. Speed and responsiveness.
2. Clean pre approvals.
3. Listing agent calls.
4. Honest communication when things slip.

**Closing beat:** Tell the room this is the door opener to 301. We will spend a whole session on partners.

---

## Slide 8. Confidence without fake hype

**Say live:**
Confidence is process and clarity. Confidence is calm in the unknown. Confidence is a dated next step at the end of every call. Confidence is not overpromising. Confidence is not pretending you know what you do not know. Confidence is not quoting a rate to look smart.

**Live tone:** Slow. Make this the values slide.

**Pause point:**
PAUSE FOR LIVE Q AND A.
Question: Where do new LOs sometimes confuse confidence with overpromising?

**Listen for:**
1. Quoting a rate before underwriting.
2. Promising a closing day too early.
3. Telling a borrower the file is approved when only a soft pull happened.
4. Bashing competitors to sound strong.

**Compliance beat:** Reinforce the no production guarantee rule and no teaser rate rule.

---

## Slide 9. Activity tracking basics

**Say live:**
If you do not track the week, you cannot coach the week. Track conversations per day. Past client touches per week. Partner touches per week. Follow ups sent and answered. A dated next step on every active file. That is the whole tracker.

**Live tone:** Practical. Make it boring on purpose.

**Pause point:**
PAUSE FOR LIVE Q AND A.
Question: What are the three activities you should track every week?

**Listen for:**
1. Conversations.
2. Partner touches.
3. Follow ups.
The full tracker also includes past client touches and content output.

**Closing beat:** Tell the room the Friday review is 15 minutes. One specific change next week. That is the rhythm.

---

## Slide 10. Today's assignment

**Say live:**
Here is the assignment. Run three borrower or referral partner conversations using the script. Log who you talked to. Log what happened. Log the next step. Bring one lesson learned back to the group next time. Three conversations. Three logs. One lesson.

**Live tone:** Specific. No wiggle room.

**Closing line, repeat exactly:**
The goal is not perfection. The goal is one clean conversation, one clear next step, and one tracked activity at a time.

**Pause point:** None marked. Use remaining time for open Q and A.

**Closing beat:** Thank the room. Remind them this is internal training. Compliance review any borrower or public artifact you build off these ideas.

---

## Presenter checklist

1. Six Q and A pauses planned.
2. Allow at least 90 seconds per pause.
3. Compliance reminders called out on slides 4, 5, and 8.
4. Repeat the operating principle three times in the session.
5. Close every Q and A with a one sentence summary in your own words.
6. End on time. The last word should be the closing line.
