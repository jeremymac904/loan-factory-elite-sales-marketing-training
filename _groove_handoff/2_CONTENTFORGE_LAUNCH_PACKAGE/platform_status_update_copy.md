## Launch FAQ

**Use as:** standalone FAQ page in-app, plus paste-into-email when users ask the same question twice.

### 1. How do I sign in?
```
Go to https://loan-factory-elite-sales-marketing-tr.netlify.app and sign in
with the email Jeremy has on file for you. If you didn't get a welcome
email, ping Jeremy on Google Chat. Your role and department are already
set — you don't need to configure anything to get in.
```

### 2. What role am I?
```
Your role is shown in the top right of your dashboard next to your name.
Roles on the platform: master_admin, admin, lo_development_lead,
lo_development_member, loan_officer_support, corporate_coach, marketing,
team_leader, coaching_member_level_1 ($249 LO Mastery),
coaching_member_level_2 ($449 Alliance), loan_officer. If your role looks
wrong, ping Jeremy — he'll fix it from /admin/users.
```

### 3. What is View-As?
```
View-As is a trainer-only tool that lets admins and LO Development leads
preview the platform exactly as one of your users sees it. It's read-only
on their behalf — it will not send messages, post to FaceGram, or run
their AI Twin. Use it to confirm what a user is seeing before walking them
through a fix. Where: /admin/users → pick a user → "View As".
```

### 4. Who do I ask for help?
```
- Platform / sign-in / role / Twin issues → Jeremy
- Training / coaching / Power Hour / recordings → Andre
- Coaching tier billing or access questions → your assigned coach, then Jeremy
- Live-deal issues → your normal channels first, then the Lender Escalation
  form inside the platform if you need it escalated
```

### 5. Can my AI Twin send emails?
```
No. Your AI Twin writes drafts in your voice. You review every draft. You
send it yourself from your normal email tool. There is no auto-send, no
scheduled send, and no bulk send. It is scoped to you — your Twin cannot
act for anyone else and no one else's Twin can act for you.
```

### 6. Where do I find recordings?
```
All session recordings — Power Hour, biweekly group coaching, mastermind
breakouts, team leader pilot — live in the Training Library. Coaching
session recaps land within 24 hours of the live session. Use the subgroup
filter to jump straight to what you need.
```

### 7. What's the difference between LO Mastery and Alliance?
```
LO Mastery ($249) is the daily coaching home — Power Hour, biweekly group
coaching, daily coaching email, Certified Mortgage Advisor track, scripts,
trackers, resources, leaderboard, and the LO Mastery Coaching Assistant.

Alliance ($449) is everything in LO Mastery plus Daily Breakfast Club,
weekly coaching, biweekly Mastermind, advanced certifications, priority
coaching, the leadership and team builder track, mastermind resources,
and the Alliance Coaching Assistant.

Ready to move up to Alliance? Talk to your coach in your next session.
```

### 8. What is FaceGram?
```
FaceGram is the platform's internal community feed and messaging layer for
approved Loan Factory users. Post wins, ask questions, share resources,
DM another user, save posts to /facegram/saved, and get notifications when
people reply or mention you. It is internal-only — nothing on FaceGram is
public. Treat it like a group chat that's actually searchable.
```

## Where to Start — 4-Step Quick Start for Normal LOs

**Use as:** in-app onboarding card on first sign-in, and bottom of the Welcome email.

```
Where to start (4 steps, about 15 minutes):

1. Update your profile at /profile/edit
   Name, title, photo, market, bio. This is how the rest of the platform sees
   you on FaceGram and how your Twin reads your voice defaults.

2. Set up your AI Twin at /ai-assistants/setup
   5 steps, about 10 minutes. Pick your voice profile, paste 3 sample notes,
   run your first draft.

3. Open the Training Library and pick one clip
   Pick a 5–10 minute clip that matches what you're stuck on this week. Watch
   it. Run the move on your next call.

4. Post one thing on FaceGram
   A win, a question, or a script that worked. The feed gets useful when
   actual people post. Be one of them.

That's day one. The rest will surface itself as you use the platform.
```
