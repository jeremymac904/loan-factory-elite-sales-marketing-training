# 2\. STEP-BY-STEP WALKTHROUGH

**Goal**

Connect your Facebook Business Page to the Loan Factory system and launch your first Facebook Lead Ad so that new leads flow automatically into your Loan Factory pipeline.

**Who This Is For**

Any active Loan Factory loan officer who wants to run paid Facebook ads and receive leads directly inside their Loan Factory account.

**Before You Start**

You must have a Facebook Business Page. A personal profile alone will not work. If you only have a personal profile, you need to create a Facebook Business Page first. You can manage multiple pages through your personal profile, but the page itself must exist before you begin this process.

**Required Accounts**

A Loan Factory system login and an active Facebook Business Page are both required. A personal Facebook account that owns or has full admin access to the Business Page is also required.

**Exact Navigation Path**

Log into your Loan Factory account. In the left menu, click Marketing Features, then click Facebook Ads. You can also go directly to www.loanfactory.com/facebook_ads.

\---

**Step-by-Step Instructions**

Step 1 - Access Facebook Ads in the Loan Factory System

Log into your Loan Factory account. In the left navigation menu, click Marketing Features, then select Facebook Ads. You can also go directly to www.loanfactory.com/facebook_ads. You will land on the Facebook Ads page. Click Connect with Facebook.

Step 2 - Connect Your Facebook Profile Account

On the Facebook Ads Config screen, toggle on "Get leads from Facebook ads." Then click the "Connect to Facebook Account" button on the right side of the screen. A Meta authentication window will appear. If you are already logged into Facebook, you will see your name with a "Continue as \[Your Name\]" option. Click it. If you are not logged in, log in first, then continue.

Step 3 - Grant Page Access and Permissions

Facebook will ask you to choose which Pages you want to connect. Select "Opt in to current Pages only" and choose your Business Page. Click Continue. Facebook will then show you a permissions review screen listing what the Loan Factory system will be allowed to do (manage ads, access leads, read page content, etc.). Click Save, then click "Got it" to complete the authorization.

Step 4 - Successfully Connected - Configure Your Settings

After the connection succeeds, the Loan Factory screen will update to show the connected account. You will now see three numbered actions to complete. First (labeled 1), click the Ad account dropdown and select your Facebook Ad account. Second (labeled 2), confirm or select the Default Loan Officer for new leads - this is who receives the leads generated by ads. Third (labeled 3), click Submit.

If a pop-up appears saying "You must create a Facebook page in order to run Facebook ads," the connection has failed. Go to the Troubleshooting section below.

Step 5 - Create Your First Ad

Once connected, click "Create New Facebook Ad." Choose your ad type. Two options are available: Lead Form (captures the lead's contact info directly on Facebook) or Website Traffic (sends the user to your site). Select an ad template from the gallery. Templates include Rocket_OnePlus_1, TSAHC-Texas, Refinance 1, HELOC, and others. Click Select on the template you want.

Step 6 - Set Budget and Schedule

Enter your daily budget. The document recommends starting with $5 to $10 per day. Choose your Facebook Page to run the ad from. Set any other schedule settings you want, then continue.

Step 7 - Select Your Target Audience

Set your target State, Language, and any Detailed Targeting you want (such as home real estate interests). You can also enable a notification to your cell phone when a new lead is created. Click Submit.

Step 8 - Confirm Your Ad Is Running

Your ad campaign will appear on the Facebook Ads dashboard inside Loan Factory. You will see columns for On/Off status, Facebook Ads name, Daily Budget, Leads count, Amount Spent, Campaign name, and Status. A green "Active" tag confirms the ad is live.

Step 9 - Receive Your Leads

Leads from your Facebook Ads are automatically brought into your Loan Factory pipeline. The lead source will be tagged as "Automatically Created." You will see them in your Leads pipeline immediately after a lead submits their information through the ad.

\---

**Common Mistakes**

Using a personal Facebook profile instead of a Business Page is the most common reason the connection fails. Connecting a Facebook account that does not have admin access to the Business Page is the second most common error - the system requires the account used to connect to be an admin or full-control owner of the page. Closing the browser tab during the ad submission process can cause an incomplete submission. Do not close the tab until you see the confirmation that the ad has been created.

**How to Know It Worked**

The Facebook Ads Config screen will update to show your connected Facebook page name and ID in the Facebook page section. Your Ad account will be selectable from a dropdown. After you create an ad, it will appear in the dashboard with an "Active" status. Leads will begin appearing in your pipeline tagged as "Automatically Created."

**What to Do If It Fails**

If you receive the pop-up error "You must create a Facebook page in order to run Facebook ads," it means the Facebook account you used to connect does not have full admin access to the Business Page. You need to add a different Facebook account as a temporary administrator of your Business Page with full control, then use that account to grant full admin rights back to your personal account. The troubleshooting section of this document walks through this process in five detailed steps using Facebook's Settings & Privacy \> Page Setup \> Manage and View Access path.

**When to Ask LO Support or Marketing for Help**

Contact LO Support at it.dept@loanfactory.com if the connection error persists after completing the troubleshooting steps. Contact the Marketing team if you need help selecting the right ad template for your market, setting audience targeting, or understanding ad performance data.

# 3\. TRAINING MODULE VERSION

**Lesson Title:** Connect Your Facebook Account and Launch Your First Facebook Ad

**Subtitle:** How to link your Facebook Business Page to the Loan Factory system and start generating inbound leads through paid advertising

**Audience:** Active Loan Factory loan officers at any experience level who are ready to invest in paid digital lead generation

**Estimated Time:** 20-30 minutes to complete setup. Allow additional time if you need to create a Facebook Business Page or resolve admin access issues.

**Learning Objective:** By the end of this lesson, the LO will be able to connect their Facebook Business Page to the Loan Factory system, select an ad template, set a budget and target audience, launch a campaign, and locate new leads in their pipeline.

\---

**Step-by-Step Lesson**

Lesson Part 1: The Facebook Page Requirement

Before you do anything inside Loan Factory, confirm you have a Facebook Business Page. Log into Facebook, click your profile picture in the top right corner, and look for a Page name in your profile switcher. If you only see your personal profile, you need to create a Business Page. Facebook allows you to manage multiple Pages through your personal account. Your Business Page is what the Loan Factory system connects to - not your personal profile.

Lesson Part 2: Navigate to Facebook Ads in Loan Factory

Log into your Loan Factory account. From the left menu, click Marketing Features, then Facebook Ads. You can also go directly to www.loanfactory.com/facebook_ads. This brings you to the Facebook Ads tool within the system.

Lesson Part 3: Connect Your Facebook Account

On the Facebook Ads Config screen, toggle on "Get leads from Facebook ads." Click "Connect to Facebook Account." Facebook will open an authentication window. Click "Continue as \[Your Name\]." Select "Opt in to current Pages only" and choose your Business Page. Click Continue, then review the permissions, click Save, and click "Got it."

Lesson Part 4: Complete Your Configuration

After the successful connection, the screen will refresh showing three numbered items. Select your Ad account from dropdown 1\. Confirm the Default Loan Officer for new leads in field 2\. Click Submit for item 3\. This links your ad leads to your pipeline.

Lesson Part 5: Create and Launch Your First Ad

Click "Create New Facebook Ad." Choose Lead Form or Website Traffic. Browse the available ad templates and click Select on the one you want. Set your daily budget ($5-$10 to start is recommended). Choose your Facebook Business Page. Set your target audience by state, language, and interests. Click Submit. Your ad is now scheduled and will go live.

Lesson Part 6: Check Your Pipeline for Leads

As leads come in from your Facebook Ad, they will appear in your Loan Factory Leads pipeline. The source will be tagged "Automatically Created" so you can identify them. No manual import is needed - the system handles it automatically.

\---

**Success Checklist**

\- Facebook Business Page exists and you have full admin access to it  
\- Logged into Loan Factory and navigated to Marketing Features \>\> Facebook Ads  
\- Toggled on "Get leads from Facebook ads"  
\- Clicked "Connect to Facebook Account" and completed the Meta authorization flow  
\- Selected your Facebook Page with "Opt in to current Pages only"  
\- Granted all requested permissions and clicked Save, then "Got it"  
\- Selected Ad account and Default Loan Officer and clicked Submit  
\- Created at least one ad with a template, budget, and target audience  
\- Confirmed the ad appears in the dashboard with Active status  
\- Confirmed the Facebook Ads page shows your Business Page name and ID

\---

**Common Troubleshooting Section**

Problem: Pop-up says "You must create a Facebook page in order to run Facebook ads."  
Cause: The Facebook account you used does not have full admin control of the Business Page.  
Fix: Add a second Facebook account as an admin on your Business Page. Use that account to grant full admin rights to your personal account. Then return to Step 2 and reconnect.

Problem: I do not see my Facebook Business Page in the connection window.  
Cause: You may be logged into the wrong Facebook account, or the Business Page may not exist yet.  
Fix: Confirm you are logged into the correct Facebook account. If the page does not exist, create it at facebook.com/pages/create.

Problem: My ad was created but shows as "Deleted" in the dashboard.  
Cause: This is likely a duplicate or test campaign. Check your active campaigns and ensure at least one shows "Active" status.

Problem: I closed the browser tab while the ad was submitting.  
Fix: Return to the Facebook Ads page in Loan Factory. Check if the ad was created. If it was not, repeat the ad creation process from the template selection step.

# 4\. SALES AND MARKETING 101 TO 601 PLACEMENT

**Recommended Class: 401 Content and Marketing**

This lesson belongs in the 401 Content and Marketing class.

Here is why. The 401 level is where Loan Officers move from understanding lead generation concepts to actually executing paid and organic marketing campaigns. Setting up Facebook Ads inside the Loan Factory system is a direct execution task - it requires a platform account, a Facebook Business Page, a budget decision, an audience strategy, and creative (template) selection. It is not a foundational concept (101) or a borrower conversion skill (201). It is not about referral partner outreach (301). It is a content distribution and paid marketing execution skill.

At the 401 level, LOs are expected to have their Loan Factory profile set up, their basic digital presence established, and their marketing awareness developed enough to allocate budget toward ads. This Facebook Ads setup lesson fits perfectly as a core 401 execution module that teaches LOs how to activate one of the most scalable paid lead generation tools available inside the system.

A secondary argument could be made for 301 Referral Partner Growth if the LO is using ad campaigns to generate realtor partnership leads, but the primary application - generating direct borrower leads from paid Facebook ads - is squarely a 401 activity.

# 5\. AI ADVANTAGE PLACEMENT

**How This Lesson Connects to AI-Assisted Marketing**

This lesson is a natural entry point for introducing AI tools into the LO's marketing workflow. Once the technical connection is made and the ad system is live, AI can help the LO do more with less effort at every stage of the campaign lifecycle.

**Plan Facebook Ad Copy**

An LO can use an AI tool to generate multiple variations of ad headline and body copy for different loan products. For example, they could prompt AI with: "Write three short Facebook ad captions for a refinance campaign targeting homeowners in California who may benefit from lower rates. Keep it under 90 words and include a soft call to action." The AI produces options the LO can paste into the ad template's primary text field.

**Create Audience Ideas**

AI can help LOs think through Facebook targeting before they sit down in the system. An LO could ask: "What Facebook interest categories and demographic filters would best reach first-time homebuyers aged 28 to 40 in Texas?" The AI response gives them a starting point for their detailed targeting fields in the ad setup.

**Draft Follow-Up Messages**

When leads come in from a Facebook ad and land in the Loan Factory pipeline tagged "Automatically Created," AI can help the LO write the first contact message. Prompt example: "Write a friendly, non-pushy opening text message for a mortgage lead who just submitted their info through a Facebook ad. They are interested in a home purchase. Keep it under 50 words." This connects the marketing lesson directly to the borrower conversion skills in 201\.

**Create a Simple Campaign Checklist**

AI can help an LO build a weekly ad management routine. Prompt example: "Create a simple 5-step weekly checklist for a loan officer who is running one Facebook ad campaign on a $7/day budget. Include checking lead volume, reviewing cost per lead, and deciding when to pause or adjust the ad." This teaches the LO to use AI as an ongoing operational support tool, not just a one-time setup assistant.

**Repurpose the Training into a Short Video Script**

AI can take this document or any lesson summary and turn it into a narrated video script the LO can record for their own team or clients. Prompt example: "Turn this 4-step Facebook Ads setup summary into a 90-second screen recording script. Use a friendly, confident tone. Start with why it matters and end with a call to action." This connects directly to Section 6 of this package.
